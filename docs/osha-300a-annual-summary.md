# Annual Injury & Illness Summary (Form 300A)

Who this is for: **Company Admins** and **EHS Coordinators** managing OSHA Logs.

## What it is

OSHA Form 300A is a summary of every recordable work-related injury and illness for the
year — total case counts by category, total days away/restricted, and injury/illness type
breakdown. It does not list individual cases (that's the OSHA 300 Log); it's just the totals.

## When it's due

Every covered employer must **post Form 300A from February 1 through April 30** of the
following year, in a location where employee notices are customarily posted — even if there
were zero recordable injuries or illnesses that year. A company executive (an owner, a
corporate officer, the highest-ranking official at the establishment, or that official's
immediate supervisor) must certify it before it's posted.

## Where to find it

Go to **OSHA Logs → Annual Summary (Form 300A)**. The tab shows:

- Establishment info (name, EIN, NAICS code, average employees, total hours worked)
- Case totals by category (deaths, days-away, restricted/transfer, other recordable —
  columns G–J), and days totals (columns K–L)
- Injury/illness type breakdown (injury, skin disorder, respiratory, poisoning, hearing
  loss, other illness — columns M–R)
- TRIR and DART rate
- A "Download printable summary (PDF)" button that generates a form-matching PDF you can
  print, sign, and post

## How totals are calculated

Everything on this tab is **computed automatically** from your OSHA 300 Log entries for the
current year, combined with your establishment's average employee count and total hours
worked (set in **Settings → OSHA Establishment & Reporting**). There is no separate place to
enter 300A numbers by hand — the summary and the PDF always match the underlying log exactly,
because they're calculated from the same data at the same time.

## If a total looks wrong

**Don't edit the summary directly — there's nothing to edit.** The numbers come from the
OSHA 300 Log, so:

1. Go to the **OSHA 300 Log** tab and find the case that's miscounted (wrong classification,
   wrong injury/illness type, wrong days-away/restricted figures, or a case that's missing
   entirely).
2. Correct or add the case there.
3. Come back to the 300A tab — the totals update immediately.

If you see a red **"Some numbers don't add up"** banner, it means you have recordable cases
logged for the year but no hours-worked figure entered — rates can't be trusted until you add
it in Settings.

## Missing establishment data

If average employees or total hours worked hasn't been entered yet, you'll see an amber
**"We're missing some site details"** banner with a link straight to Settings. The PDF
download button is disabled until both fields are filled in, so you can't accidentally post an
incomplete summary.

## No injuries or illnesses this year?

If there are zero recordable cases for the year, the "No injuries or illnesses to report"
checkbox on the tab (and on the PDF) is automatically checked — you still need to post the
form with this box checked.
