CORTEXA BIOSCIENCES, INC.
INJURY & ILLNESS PREVENTION PROGRAM (IIPP) / CHEMICAL HYGIENE PLAN
Revision 3 — Effective January 2026

SECTION 3 — REGULATORY COMPLIANCE
This program is maintained in accordance with the following standards:
- OSHA Hazard Communication Standard, 29 CFR 1910.1200 — maintain Safety Data Sheets,
  container labeling, written HazCom program, and annual employee training.
- OSHA Occupational Exposure to Hazardous Chemicals in Laboratories, 29 CFR 1910.1450 —
  written Chemical Hygiene Plan and a designated Chemical Hygiene Officer (CHO).
- OSHA Formaldehyde Standard, 29 CFR 1910.1048 — initial and periodic exposure monitoring
  and a medical surveillance program for exposed employees.
- OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens, 29 CFR 1910.1030 — Exposure Control Plan and Hepatitis B
  vaccination offered to staff handling human cell lines.
- EPA Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, 40 CFR 262 — hazardous waste generator
  accumulation limits and manifesting.

SECTION 4 — REQUIRED TRAINING
All laboratory personnel must complete:
- Hazard Communication / GHS training — annual.
- Chemical Hygiene & Laboratory Safety — at hire and annual refresher.
- Bloodborne Pathogens — annual, for staff working with human-derived materials.
- Formaldehyde Safety — for histology staff prior to first exposure.

SECTION 5 — IDENTIFIED HAZARDS
- Inhalation/skin exposure to formaldehyde during tissue fixation (carcinogen) — high risk.
- Flammable solvent fire during handling and storage of methanol/acetonitrile — high risk.
- Cryogenic burns and oxygen displacement from liquid nitrogen in cryostorage — medium risk.
- Exposure to BSL-2 biological agents during cell culture — medium risk.
